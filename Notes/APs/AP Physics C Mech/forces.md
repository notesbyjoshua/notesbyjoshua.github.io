## Newton's Three Laws of Motion

In 1687, Newton formulated his three laws of motion. They work exceptionally well in most cases, but keep in mind that at quantum, relativistic, or supermassive scales, Newton's laws break down and you have to use different methods. However, for the topic of AP Physics C, Newton's three laws are the gold standard. They are

1. An object in motion, stays in motion, and an object in rest, stays in rest unless acted by an external force. This can be modeled as $$a = 0$$, if $$\vec{F} = 0$$.
2. $$\vec{F_net} = m\vec{a}$$
3. A force applied on an object will always have an equal, but opposite force. This can be modeled as $$\vec{F_a-on-b} = \vec{F_b-on-a}$$.
